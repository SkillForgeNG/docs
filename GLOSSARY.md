# SkillForge Glossary

## Foundational Concepts

### Core Learning Terms

**Competency**
A demonstrated ability to apply knowledge, skills, and attributes to successfully perform a role or task. Competencies are the cornerstone of SkillForge and represent what learners can actually do, not what they have studied.

**Skill**
A specific, measurable capability acquired through learning and practice. Skills are atomic units that combine to form competencies. Example: "HTML fundamentals" is a skill that contributes to "Frontend Development" competency.

**Evidence**
Documentation, artifacts, or performance demonstrations that prove competency achievement. Evidence includes project outputs, assessment results, peer reviews, and real-world accomplishments.

**Mastery**
The highest level of competency achievement, where a learner can not only perform a skill reliably but can also teach, mentor, and innovate within that domain.

**Certification**
An official credential recognizing that a learner has demonstrated specific competencies through evidence-based assessment. Certifications are earned, not awarded.

### Learning Design

**Learning Mission**
A cohesive, time-bound learning challenge combining curated resources, projects, and assessments. A mission guides learners toward mastery of specific skills or competencies.

**Learning Path**
A personalized sequence of learning missions, skills, and projects designed to help a learner progress toward career goals. Paths are adaptive and respond to learner progress and preferences.

**Project**
A real-world, practical challenge requiring learners to apply skills and competencies. Projects generate evidence that is used in assessment and certification.

**Module**
A collection of resources, lessons, and activities focused on teaching specific skills or sub-competencies within a learning mission.

**Lesson**
An individual unit of instruction covering a specific learning objective. Lessons combine explanations, examples, practice, and reflection.

**Assessment**
A systematic evaluation of whether a learner has achieved specific learning objectives. Assessments are evidence-based and competency-aligned.

**Resource**
Curated educational content including videos, articles, documentation, interactive tutorials, and practice exercises. Resources are linked to specific skills and learning missions.

### Learner Identity & Progress

**Skill Passport**
A comprehensive, lifelong professional profile showcasing a learner's skills, competencies, projects, evidence, assessments, achievements, and career journey. The Skill Passport is portable and owned by the learner.

**Portfolio**
A curated collection of a learner's best project work, evidence, and achievements. Portfolios demonstrate real-world impact and are often shared with employers and peers.

**Career Goal**
A clearly defined professional aspiration that guides learning path personalization. Career goals drive recommendation and adaptive learning systems.

**Study Plan**
A learner's personalized roadmap generated by AI, integrating career goals, current competencies, available time, and learning preferences.

**Progress**
Quantifiable advancement through learning paths and toward competency mastery. Progress is measured by evidence collection, assessment completion, and project delivery.

**Learning History**
A complete record of a learner's educational journey, including missions completed, skills acquired, assessments passed, and competencies achieved.

### Roles & Relationships

**Learner**
Any individual acquiring, developing, and demonstrating competencies through the SkillForge platform. Learners are self-directed agents of their learning.

**Instructor**
An educator or subject matter expert who creates learning missions, assesses evidence, provides feedback, and mentors learners.

**Administrator**
A platform operator responsible for system configuration, user management, organizational settings, and content governance.

**Organization**
A group entity (company, school, non-profit) using SkillForge to develop learner competencies at scale. Organizations can customize learning paths and competency frameworks.

**Employer**
A future user of SkillForge who will recruit and assess talent using verified skill passports and competency evidence.

**Mentor**
A future role: an experienced professional guiding and supporting learners on their competency journey.

### Platform Architecture

**Bounded Context**
A clear boundary in the domain model where specific business logic, rules, and entities apply. Bounded contexts communicate through well-defined interfaces and shared language (Ubiquitous Language).

**Ubiquitous Language**
The shared vocabulary used within a bounded context by developers, designers, and domain experts. This language eliminates ambiguity and aligns implementation with business intent.

**Entity**
A domain object with continuous identity throughout its lifecycle, distinguished by its uniqueness rather than its attributes.

**Value Object**
An immutable domain object defined by its attributes, not its identity. Examples: a skill level rating or a competency badge.

**Aggregate**
A cluster of domain objects (entities and value objects) treated as a single unit of consistency and change.

**Recommendation**
AI-generated personalized suggestions for learning missions, skills, projects, or career paths based on learner profile, goals, and progress.

## Philosophical Principles

**Competency-Based Learning**
Education organized around demonstrated ability rather than seat time or content consumption. Learning is complete when competency is proven through evidence.

**Project-Based Learning**
Learning through authentic, challenging, real-world projects that require application of multiple skills and generate portfolio-worthy evidence.

**Self-Directed Learning**
Learners taking ownership of their educational journey, choosing learning paths, setting goals, and driving their own progress.

**Evidence-Based Assessment**
Evaluation grounded in tangible demonstrations of competency, not assumptions. Evidence is collected continuously and used to guide adaptive learning.

**AI-Assisted Learning**
AI systems augmenting human learning and instruction, providing personalization, recommendations, and guidance—but never replacing critical thinking or human judgment.

**Mastery Learning**
The commitment to ensuring all learners can achieve mastery through persistence, adaptive support, and re-engagement until competency is demonstrated.

## Language & Accessibility

**First-Class Language**
English and Hausa are treated equally in design, content, and user experience—not as afterthoughts or translations.

**Accessibility**
Inclusion by design: SkillForge is built from the start to serve learners with diverse abilities, devices, and connectivity constraints.

**Localization**
Adapting SkillForge for regional, cultural, and linguistic contexts beyond simple translation, including competency frameworks and career pathways.

## Strategic Concepts

**Talent Ecosystem**
A future vision where employers, educators, learners, and platforms converge to create a seamless, evidence-based labor market.

**Skills Infrastructure**
A proposed public utility providing verified, portable, lifetime skill credentials across institutional and geographic boundaries.

**Continuous Improvement**
The commitment to refining SkillForge based on learner outcomes, instructor feedback, and emerging market needs.

*This glossary is the definitive reference for SkillForge terminology. All documentation, designs, and implementations must use these terms consistently.*
